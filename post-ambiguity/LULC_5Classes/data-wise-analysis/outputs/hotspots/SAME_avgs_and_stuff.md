These statistics perfectly describe Ribbon Development.

    Distance to Roads: The median distance for new urban growth is just 607 meters from a major road. The standard deviation (920m) is relatively tight, showing that urbanization strongly clusters around transportation networks rather than spreading randomly into rural farmland.

As expected, natural river movements do not care about human infrastructure.

    Elevation: The median elevation for river shifts is 45.5 meters (mean: 54m). Notice how this is substantially lower than the urban areas. Furthermore, the standard deviation is very low (only 26.9m). This mathematically proves that water volatility is strictly confined to the absolute lowest, flattest geographical depressions in the state (the active Gangetic/Kosi floodplains).

    Distance to Roads: The average distance to a major road is 1,601 meters, with a massive standard deviation of 1,434 meters. This huge spread tells us there is absolutely no spatial relationship between where rivers shift and where roads are built. The river movement is completely independent of human factors.
